How about we

savor the scenery of the Eastern Seaboard on a bike tour (with a vineyard wine tasting).
Use keyboard

Built for two

"Chasing records doesn't keep me on my bike. Happiness does." ~ Lance Armstrong

The world just seems a little sweeter when you're on a bike. Stopping that bike to sip a little wine at North Fork's renowned vineyards can only help. Add the ocean breeze blowing off the Long Island Sound to the mix, and it would be impossible to make it any more memorable.

You’ll be biking on back roads through lush green countryside lined with rows of grapevines; you’ll take breaks at farm stands where you can buy berries and fresh baked fruit pies straight from local producers. You’ll also stop at the beach in North Fork, where you can dig your toes into the fine white sand (or dip them in the ocean).

One of a kind

The cured pickles from Artisinal Pickle are brined in a secret sauce.

On the date

  • You’ll be driven to Long Island’s gorgeous North Fork area.
  • Your ride starts at Macari Vineyards in Mattituck.
  • You’ll stop along the way for wine tastings, fresh berries and baked goods.
  • Snap photos at attractions like Horton's Point Lighthouse; draw in the sand when you hit the beach on the North Fork.

Made with love

Discover Outdoors founder Jack Outdoorsman grew up in Long Island, went to school in Colorado, then moved to Manhattan in his early twenties.

Working at a TK, he quickly grew frustrated with how little his colleagues and girlfriend knew about the sprawling nature just out of reach.

Verena and Yolanda Founders, Discover Outdoors

The details

This date takes place on Sunday, July 22nd.

You’ll meet at 7:45am in Union Square, NYC, and you’ll be back in the city by about 6:45pm.

Bring your bike, or rent one from Discover Outdoors.

Wear athletic clothing that’s comfortable for riding

The roads are primarily paved back roads, and the route is 18 miles long with rolling hills.

You don’t need to be an expert rider, but you should be comfortable on a bike.

This trip is a “Level 2” Intensity Level out of 5 -- good for active first-timers.
